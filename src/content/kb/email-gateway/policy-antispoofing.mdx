# Anti-Spoofing Policy

Email spoofing is one of the most prevalent attack vectors, allowing attackers to impersonate legitimate senders. NextGuard Email Security Gateway enforces **SPF**, **DKIM**, and **DMARC** authentication standards to verify sender identity and protect your domain from being used in phishing and fraud campaigns.

---

## Understanding Email Authentication

| Standard | Full Name | What It Validates |
|----------|-----------|-------------------|
| SPF | Sender Policy Framework | Authorized sending IP addresses for a domain |
| DKIM | DomainKeys Identified Mail | Cryptographic signature of message integrity |
| DMARC | Domain-based Message Authentication, Reporting & Conformance | Policy enforcement based on SPF/DKIM alignment |

All three work together:
- **SPF** tells receiving servers which IPs are allowed to send email for your domain
- **DKIM** adds a digital signature to verify the message was not tampered with in transit
- **DMARC** defines what to do when SPF/DKIM checks fail and where to send reports

---

## SPF Configuration

### How SPF Works

1. Your domain publishes a DNS TXT record listing authorized mail servers
2. The receiving server checks if the sending IP matches the SPF record
3. Result: Pass, Fail, SoftFail, Neutral, or None

### Publishing Your SPF Record

Add a TXT record to your domain's DNS:

```dns
v=spf1 ip4:203.0.113.10 ip4:203.0.113.20 include:_spf.nextguard.com ~all
```

| Parameter | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| `v=spf1` | SPF version identifier |
| `ip4:x.x.x.x` | Authorized IP address |
| `include:domain` | Include another domain's SPF record |
| `~all` | SoftFail for unauthorized senders (recommended for testing) |
| `-all` | Fail for unauthorized senders (recommended for production) |

### Configuring SPF Checking on NextGuard

1. Navigate to **Policy > Anti-Spoofing > SPF**
2. Enable **SPF Checking**
3. Configure actions per SPF result:
   - **Pass:** Deliver normally
   - **SoftFail (~all):** Tag subject with `[SPF-SOFTFAIL]` or quarantine
   - **Fail (-all):** Reject or quarantine
   - **None (no SPF record):** Tag or pass (depends on risk tolerance)
4. Enable **SPF Score Boost:** Add +30 to spam score on Fail
5. Click **Save**

## DKIM Configuration

### How DKIM Works

1. Outbound emails are signed with a private key stored on your mail server
2. The public key is published in your DNS as a TXT record
3. Receiving servers retrieve the public key and verify the signature

### Setting Up DKIM Signing (Outbound)

1. Navigate to **Policy > Anti-Spoofing > DKIM > Signing**
2. Click **Generate DKIM Key Pair**
3. Configure:
   - **Domain:** `yourdomain.com`
   - **Selector:** `mail` (becomes `mail._domainkey.yourdomain.com`)
   - **Key size:** 2048-bit (recommended)
4. Click **Generate**
5. Copy the generated **public key TXT record**
6. Publish to DNS:
```dns
mail._domainkey.yourdomain.com  TXT  "v=DKIM1; k=rsa; p=MIIBIjANBgkq..."
```
7. Click **Enable DKIM Signing**
8. Click **Save**

### Configuring DKIM Verification (Inbound)

1. Navigate to **Policy > Anti-Spoofing > DKIM > Verification**
2. Enable **DKIM Verification** for inbound email
3. Configure actions:
   - **DKIM Pass:** Deliver normally, reduce spam score by 10
   - **DKIM Fail:** Add +20 to spam score or quarantine
   - **DKIM None (no signature):** No penalty (many legitimate senders don't use DKIM)
4. Click **Save**

### DKIM Key Rotation

Rotate DKIM keys every 6–12 months:
1. Generate a new key pair with a new selector (e.g., `mail2`)
2. Publish the new public key to DNS
3. Switch signing to the new key
4. Leave the old DNS record in place for 48–72 hours (TTL propagation)
5. Remove the old DNS record after propagation

## DMARC Configuration

### How DMARC Works

DMARC builds on SPF and DKIM by adding:
- **Alignment checking:** Requires SPF/DKIM domains to align with the `From:` header domain
- **Policy enforcement:** Specifies what to do with failing emails (none/quarantine/reject)
- **Reporting:** Sends aggregate and forensic reports to specified email addresses

### Publishing Your DMARC Record

Add a TXT record at `_dmarc.yourdomain.com`:

```dns
_dmarc.yourdomain.com  TXT  "v=DMARC1; p=quarantine; rua=mailto:dmarc-reports@yourdomain.com; ruf=mailto:dmarc-forensic@yourdomain.com; pct=100; adkim=r; aspf=r"
```

| Parameter | Value | Description |
|-----------|-------|-------------|
| `v=DMARC1` | Required | Version identifier |
| `p=` | none/quarantine/reject | Policy for failing emails |
| `rua=` | mailto: address | Aggregate report destination |
| `ruf=` | mailto: address | Forensic report destination |
| `pct=` | 1–100 | Percentage of mail to apply policy to |
| `adkim=` | r (relaxed) / s (strict) | DKIM alignment mode |
| `aspf=` | r (relaxed) / s (strict) | SPF alignment mode |

### DMARC Deployment Strategy

Deploy DMARC gradually to avoid blocking legitimate email:

**Phase 1: Monitor (p=none)**
```dns
"v=DMARC1; p=none; rua=mailto:dmarc@yourdomain.com"
```
- No email is blocked
- Aggregate reports reveal all sending sources
- Duration: 2–4 weeks

**Phase 2: Quarantine (p=quarantine)**
```dns
"v=DMARC1; p=quarantine; pct=25; rua=mailto:dmarc@yourdomain.com"
```
- Start with `pct=25` (25% of failing email quarantined)
- Gradually increase to `pct=100`
- Duration: 2–4 weeks

**Phase 3: Reject (p=reject)**
```dns
"v=DMARC1; p=reject; rua=mailto:dmarc@yourdomain.com"
```
- All failing email is rejected
- Maximum protection against domain spoofing

### Configuring DMARC Enforcement on NextGuard

1. Navigate to **Policy > Anti-Spoofing > DMARC**
2. Enable **DMARC Policy Enforcement**
3. Configure:
   - **Honor DMARC p=quarantine:** Move to quarantine queue
   - **Honor DMARC p=reject:** Reject at SMTP level
   - **Override policy for internal senders:** Optional bypass for internal mail
4. Enable **DMARC Reporting:** Forward aggregate reports to `dmarc@yourdomain.com`
5. Click **Save**

## Display Name Spoofing Protection

Attackers may use a legitimate-looking display name while using a different email address.

### Configuring Display Name Protection

1. Navigate to **Policy > Anti-Spoofing > Display Name Protection**
2. Enable **Display Name Spoofing Detection**
3. Add protected identities:
   - Executive names: `CEO Name`, `CFO Name`, `IT Admin`
   - Associated email: `ceo@yourdomain.com`
4. Set action when display name matches but email domain differs:
   - **Quarantine** (recommended)
   - **Add warning banner to email**
   - **Reject**
5. Click **Save**

---

## Lookalike Domain Protection

Protects against domains visually similar to yours (e.g., `nextgu4rd.com`, `nextguard-secure.com`).

1. Navigate to **Policy > Anti-Spoofing > Lookalike Domains**
2. Enable **Lookalike Domain Detection**
3. Add your primary domain(s) to monitor
4. The system automatically flags emails from:
   - Typosquatting domains
   - Homograph attacks (Unicode characters)
   - Hyphenated variants
5. Set action: **Quarantine** or **Reject**
6. Click **Save**

## Inbound Anti-Spoofing Rules Summary

| Check | Fail Action | Recommended Setting |
|-------|------------|---------------------|
| SPF Fail (-all) | Reject | Reject |
| SPF SoftFail (~all) | Quarantine | Quarantine + Tag |
| DKIM Fail | Score boost +20 | Quarantine if score > 70 |
| DMARC p=reject | Reject | Honor policy |
| DMARC p=quarantine | Quarantine | Honor policy |
| Display Name Spoof | Quarantine | Quarantine + Alert |
| Lookalike Domain | Quarantine | Quarantine |

---

## Outbound Email Signing Summary

Ensure all outbound email is properly authenticated:

1. **SPF:** Publish SPF record with `-all` for all sending domains
2. **DKIM:** Enable DKIM signing on the NextGuard gateway
3. **DMARC:** Start with `p=none`, progress to `p=reject`
4. **Subdomains:** Add DMARC records for all subdomains (`sp=reject`)

## Monitoring and Reports

### DMARC Aggregate Reports

- Sent daily by receiving mail servers
- Contains: source IPs, SPF/DKIM pass/fail counts, policy applied
- Analyze using a DMARC report analyzer tool

### Viewing Anti-Spoofing Logs

1. Navigate to **Reports > Anti-Spoofing**
2. View:
   - SPF check results by sender domain
   - DKIM verification results
   - DMARC policy actions taken
   - Display name spoofing detections
